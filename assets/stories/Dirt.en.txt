Oh come on, I don't think it's a problem — it's not as if I'm obsessed!

You know perfectly well I like cleaning; quite simply, when I see something dirty, I cannot help but have to put everything in order, until it is white and pure. Come on, don't look at me with those eyes; don't you remember, for instance, the dog's plush toy I gave such a good cleaning to a few months ago? I think it began there. True, it ended up a bit damaged, but it came out spotless and pristine!

Then it was the lamp's turn; I had never seen so much dust collected in a single place. I could hardly leave it like that, could I? I scrubbed it thoroughly until not a trace of even the most microscopic germ remained. Yes, I know — I broke the bulb and cut myself while cleaning it; but it was an accident!

After that, my attention settled on that rug. My God, the germs that nested inside it; the dirt had become an integral part of its fibres, and so I could not bear it. I could feel millions of germs swarming between the knots of the weave; they seemed to be marching across the floor like so many tiny soldiers. I even broke my nails cleaning it as it deserved. I lost some blood, but it was entirely worth it.

What's that you say? The dog? Oh, well — that was the bare minimum… After that day at the park, he was a living agglomerate of germs.

Come on, you can't keep sulking at me. We took him out for a walk, he got himself filthy in the mud: he was covered in that disgusting stuff. When we finally brought him home, I couldn't bear it. Yes, we washed him, but I kept feeling the presence of those tiny, malevolent little things… You can't blame me for putting him in the oven — he needed it so badly!

Later, it was your turn. I felt that the germs were nesting on your skin. You always told me you were taking your shower, but in truth it was no use at all. Look how splendid you are now! After a nice bath in acid only your bones remain, but they don't have the slightest speck of dirt!

You are completely white and pure, with no trace of corruption.

But — what is this feeling? Could it be that they are on me too? Will I ever find peace from these germs? I really do think I'm in need of a nice bath…
